Why YouTube Shorts?

UX & business decisions behind short-form vertical video

Understanding the product thinking that shaped a format now central to modern video platforms.

1

Business decisions

  • Compete with TikTok & Reels — Capture younger users and watch time in short-form.
  • New revenue stream — Shorts ads (in-feed) add another monetization layer.
  • Keep creators on YouTube — Give short-form creators a home instead of losing them to other apps.
  • Funnel to long-form — Shorts tease full videos and drive subscriptions and watch time.
  • Habit & retention — Quick, low-commitment clips encourage daily opens and more time on platform.
2

UX decisions

  • Full-screen vertical — One video at a time; matches how people hold the phone.
  • Swipe for next — Infinite scroll, minimal friction, easy to binge.
  • Short length (≤60s) — Low commitment; algorithm learns preferences fast.
  • Sound on by default — Design assumes audio is part of the experience.
  • Minimal overlay UI — Like, comment, share without leaving the video.
  • Dedicated Shorts tab — Clear separation from the main subscription feed.
  • Algorithmic feed — Personalized order to maximize retention.
  • Link to channel / long-form — Shorts as a funnel to subscriptions and longer content.
3

Takeaway

UX and business are aligned: each design choice (vertical, swipe, length, feed, links) supports engagement, retention, and revenue while competing with other short-form apps. Isolate one part of the experience, explain the UX rationale, then tie it to the business goal.
